SECTION ONE Expectation of the Messiah

The glad tidings of the coming of the era of justice and divine rule are abundantly found in the sacred texts of the Jews and are among their core beliefs. According to Ernest Renan, any research into Judaism that ignores belief in the appearance of the Messiah remains incomplete. The belief in the appearance of the Messiah is inseparably tied to repentance.

When all people repent, the era of the Messiah arrives. According to Maimonides, in that era there will be no hunger, war, envy, or rivalry; all human needs will be easily fulfilled, and people will devote themselves solely to knowing God and worshipping Him.

Messiah (Mashiach)

A savior figure whose name appears in the sayings of prophets and in Jewish religious, legal, and philosophical literature. It is stated in detail that in the future, for the Jewish people, a leader will arise who is called the "True Messiah" (Mashiach Tzidki) and also "Son of David" (Mashiach ben David).

Author's Concluding Observation

What is observed in the selected passages from the Jewish sacred books is the announcement of the end of corruption, the purification of the world, and the final salvation of humanity through a divine government of justice.

Christians have claimed that the Promised One is none other than Jesus son of Mary. However, Jewish scriptures never speak of a local or temporary rule, but of a global and everlasting dominion.

The truth is this: Some of these signs refer to Jesus Christ, others to Muhammad (peace be upon him and his family), and others clearly point to Imam Mahdi.

According to Islamic narrations, the global uprising of Imam Mahdi will coincide with the return of Jesus Christ, and at that time the promises of justice and salvation will reach their fulfillment.
